Navigating the Grandview Exchange Neighborhood

Think of Grandview Exchange like a digital neighborhood made up of a main hub and smaller communities called subgroups.

Overview

The Grandview Exchange Home Group acts as the main street of the community. From there, residents can explore smaller subgroup “houses” that focus on special interests, clubs, and activities.

1. Starting at the hub: Grandview Exchange Home Group

The Home Group is the center of daily community activity. It contains community-wide news, announcements, general conversation, and help and support for using the site.

  • Community news and announcements
  • General community discussion
  • Help and support for the platform

2. Finding and joining subgroups

Subgroups are smaller dedicated spaces, organized around clubs or interests. To find them, use the Subgroups link on the left side of the Home page and browse the list.

  • Subgroups you belong to
  • Subgroups available to join

Some are open interest groups, while club subgroups may require membership or leader approval.

3. Moving between groups

When you belong to several groups, use the “Your Groups” page to see them in one place. It is available from the blue header at the top of most pages.

4. Inside each house: categories

Each subgroup organizes messages into categories, which act like rooms inside the house. Categories make it easier to find conversations about specific topics.

Think of the subgroup as the house and the category as the room inside it.
