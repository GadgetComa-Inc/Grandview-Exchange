Here is a complete list of all groups and categories (a.k.a hashtags) used in each subgroup.

**Grandview Exchange Home Group**

This is the main group, the place to discuss the Grandview Exchange and life at The Grandview Bethesda. Think of it as Main Street - the gathering place for the community. Categories include:

- Announcements and important messages about Grandview Exchange and The Grandview Bethesda (#Announcements)
- Messages from a Grandview club that would be of interest toall Grandview Exchange members (#Club-Notices)
- Events at The Grandview Bethesda (#Grandview-Events)
- Questions, comments, and feedback on Grandview Exchange (#Grandview-Exchange)
- Questions, comments, recommendations, and general discussion about life at The Grandview Bethesda (#Grandview-Living)

**Club Subgroups**

Club subgroups are spaces dedicated to Grandview clubs. Club leaders established their own categories and hashtags to fit the club's focus.

**[Bocce Club](https://grandviewexchange.groups.io/g/bocceclub)**

- The Bocce Club is designed to bring residents together, foster connections, and create memorable experiences through fun and exercise. We invite residents of all ages, from 60 to 100, to join us in this excellent venture. Participate as much or as little as you’d like–your involvement is always your choice. But we hope to “See you on the court!”

**[Bridge Club](https://grandviewexchange.groups.io/g/Bridgeclub)**

- The Bridge Club is designed to bring residents together, foster connections, and create memorable experiences through playing bridge. With our tagline, “Let’s Play!,” we invite residents of all ages—ranging from 60 to 100—to join us in this excellent venture. Participate as much or as little as you’d like—your involvement is always your choice.

**[Computer/IT Club](https://grandviewexchange.groups.io/g/ComputerClub)**

- The Club is a forum for members to become more knowledgeable about the use of their electronic devices–such as computers, laptops, tablets (like iPads), and smartphones. The Club’s members range from novices on tech issues to people who have been in the tech business or who have been speakers on tech and AI topics at local senior educational centers, such as the Osher Life-Long Learning Institutes at American University and Johns Hopkins.

**[Current Events Club](https://grandviewexchange.groups.io/g/current-events)**

- The Current Events Club is designed to bring residents together and help foster connections through stimulating discussions on topics of the day.  The Grandview is filled with residents from the DC area and from around the globe with varied and fascinating backgrounds—government service, the media, World Bank, politics, non-profits, education and the legal and healthcare communities.  We have found that many residents are interested in discussing world events, sharing their expertise and learning from each other.  

**[Fiction Book Club Lobby](https://grandviewexchange.groups.io/g/Book-Fiction-LOBBY)**

- **Interest in participating in the original Grandview Fiction Book Club quickly grew beyond its capacity once Grandview opened. Clearly, our residents are avid readers and want to share their insights and reviews with their neighbors. To facilitate active, meaningful discussions, our Clubs determined that 15 was the optimal size. As a result, current fiction clubs cannot accommodate new participants but want to encourage the creation of additional clubs to meet demand.**

**[Mah Jongg Club](https://grandviewexchange.groups.io/g/mahjongg)**

- A Mah Jongg Club is at The Grandview, and all residents are warmly invited to join in the fun. Our weekly games on Wednesdays from 1-3 pm will take place in the card room, creating a lively gathering spot where strategy, laughter, and friendly competition come together.  Mah Jongg is more than just a game—it’s a wonderful way to keep the mind active and give those brain cells a little rejuvenation each week. Just as importantly, it’s an opportunity to build camaraderie, enjoy good conversation, and strengthen friendships with neighbors around the table.

**[Modern American Canasta](https://grandviewexchange.groups.io/g/modern-canasta)**

- Good friends, good times, good canasta! We invite residents of all ages to join in on the fun. Play as often as you like, your involvement is your choice. The group is meeting every Monday, from noon to 2:00 pm at the Card Room in Potomac Heights. We are playing by the rules endorsed by the Canasta League of Americal (CLA).   The Canasta Junction website ([https://canastajunction.com](https://canastajunction.com/)) as well as the mobile Canasta Junction application, available on the Apple App Store and Google Play, are excellent places to practice based on the CLA rules. Canasta Junction is free for solitaire use.

**[Non-Fiction Book Club](https://grandviewexchange.groups.io/g/Book-Non-Fiction)**

- The Non-Fiction Book Club has been a fantastic group, usually about a dozen people, who have read about a variety of subjects; the members choose our books, & have included “The Demon of Unrest” by Erik Larson, “Say Nothing” by Patrick Radden Keefe, “Born a Crime” by Trevor Noah, “The Secret Women of the CIA” by Liza Mundy, “The Art  Thief” by Michael Finkel, All the Beauty in the World-The Metropolitan Museum and Me” by Patrick Brinkley, “Pathogenesis: A History of the World in Eight Plagues” by Jonathan Kennedy . . . and many more, as we started meeting monthly (2nd Monday of the month, at 11 AM) in late 2023.

**[Pickleball Club](https://grandviewexchange.groups.io/g/pickleballclub)**

- Welcome to the Grandview Pickleball Club, open to everyone, ages 60 to 100, with the simple motto: “Play Pickle!.”  Whether you're an experienced player or a beginner, you can play at your own pace and enjoy a fun, supportive community. Members are always happy to assist and ensure everyone can participate - from teaching some basics, running practice drills or organizing games. 

**[Social Singles Group](https://grandviewexchange.groups.io/g/singles-social-group)**

- The Singles Social Group is designed to bring residents together, foster connections, and create memorable experiences through fun and exercise. With our tagline, “Be the first to say, HELLO”, we invite residents of all ages—ranging from 60 to 100—to join us in this excellent venture. Participate as much or as little as you’d like—your involvement is always your choice.

**[TV Club](https://grandviewexchange.groups.io/g/tvclub)**

- At The Grandview, the joy of watching great stories unfold on screen is best shared with friends. The Grandview TV Club is designed to bring residents together, foster connections, and create memorable experiences through the magic of television and film. With our tagline, “Watching Together,” we invite residents of all ages—ranging from 60 to 100—to join us in this exciting new venture. (When and where you watch the “selected” program is always your choice. Join others only when you wish.)
